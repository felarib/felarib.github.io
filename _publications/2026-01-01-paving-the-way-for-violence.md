This paper builds on the dissertation's "logistical violence premium" finding, showing that infrastructure construction under Brazil's Growth Acceleration Program (PAC) increased homicides in treated municipalities. The paper is currently under revise-and-resubmit at *Latin American Politics and Society*, with revisions re-estimating the staggered difference-in-differences design using modern estimators (LP-DiD, Callaway–Sant'Anna, Sun–Abraham) and adding a qualitative account of PAC's project-assignment mechanism.
